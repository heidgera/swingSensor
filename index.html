Compressed air energy storage is a promising technology which may allow us to store energy gathered from renewable sources for later use.

However, it's not a perfect storage solution; compressing air generates heat which might be lost, and expanding compressed air to reclaim energy requires heat to reach max efficiency.

If the heat from compression can be stored for use during expansion, the efficiency of the system can increase dramatically.

Use the air compressors to either side to explore one way that researchers are trying to store heat for later use.
This compressor use a column of water to compress the air inside the cylinder. As you pull down on the handle, the water level rises, and compresses the same amount of air into a smaller and smaller volume, which increases pressure.

In this compressor, there is a mesh which helps capture heat from the air during compression, and stores it in the water. As the air expands, this heat returns from the water, allowing more energy to be reclaimed from the compressed air.
